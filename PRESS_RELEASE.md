# Hidden Networks Revealed in Epstein Case Data: A Small Number of Individuals Appear Across a Disproportionate Share of Epstein Case Records

## Name co-occurrence in a large document corpus can reveal social network structure without explicit relationship data.

## Problem Statement

The 2025 release of the Epstein files produced one of the largest public document corpora ever made available for analysis. Journalists and researchers have begun exploring the data, but most approaches rely on keyword search or manual review, which are methods that do not scale to millions of pages. This project asks a specific question: which names appear together most frequently across documents, and what does that pattern reveal about the structure of Epstein's social network?

## Solution Description 

I processed the Epstein documents to find which names appear together most often. Names that frequently show up in the same documents are likely connected. The more often two people co-occur, the stronger I treat their relationship. The result is a network map showing who is most central to Epstein's social circle based purely on the documentary record.
